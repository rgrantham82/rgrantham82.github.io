About Me

I am Robert Grantham, a calligrapher and artist with a passion for blending modern calligraphy with street art influences. Currently, I am homeless, but I haven’t allowed my circumstances to prevent me from expressing my art. Instead, I channel my experiences into creativity, using materials I can find around me.

My primary medium is cardboard. Not only is it accessible and often found discarded, but using cardboard as my canvas also aligns with my commitment to environmental sustainability. By reusing and repurposing cardboard that might otherwise end up in landfills, I aim to give new life to a material that many consider disposable. It’s my way of merging my art with a sense of purpose—turning something often overlooked into something beautiful, meaningful, and lasting.

My work is a tribute to friendship, urban life, resilience, and eco-consciousness. Every piece I create carries with it the story of not only my own journey but also the message that art can inspire and bring hope, regardless of the circumstances. I want my art to remind people that beauty can come from unexpected places and that it’s possible to make a positive impact on the environment, even in small ways.
