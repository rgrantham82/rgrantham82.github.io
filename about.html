About the artist

Robert Grantham

Austin‑based calligrapher and painter blending Copperplate & Blackletter with contemporary abstraction. I build layered, text‑driven paintings about hope, resilience, and memory—handwritten lines woven into paint.

View Portfolio Contact / Commissions

Artist Statement

I work where disciplined letterforms meet gestural paint. Years of study in Copperplate and Blackletter inform each piece: grids, stroke order, and pressure‑release—then weathered with glazes, scumbles, and palimpsests of text. Phrases such as misericordia, virtus, and resilientia recur as anchors for grief, courage, and care. The ethos is simple: “Hope, written by hand.”

Materials & Process

  • Surfaces: Ampersand Gessobord (7/8" cradle), canvas, and reclaimed cardboard.
  • Media: Acrylics with layered glazes & scumbles; occasional tempera and mixed‑media markers.
  • Lettering: Copperplate, Blackletter & calligraffiti hybrids on sacred‑geometry grids.
  • Palette: Deep blues, bone‑white / plaster neutrals, and restrained metallics.

Themes

Resilience—personal and communal—sits at the center. Magnolia motifs, Latin mottos, and German inscriptions function as talismans. I’m interested in how handwriting—our most human mark—carries memory across surfaces and years.

Commissions

I take a limited number of commissions each month—from compact calligraphy studies to larger statement works on Gessobord. If a word, motto, or concept matters to you, I’ll develop a layered design with script studies and a materials plan.

  1. Conversation: your word(s), mood, palette, size, and budget.
  2. Design packet: thumbnail sketches + lettering studies.
  3. Production: layered ground, inscription, finishing, COA.

Student / community discounts available when possible.

Pricing (guidance)

Pricing reflects square‑inch value, materials, and complexity. Typical ranges:

  • 8×10×5/8 canvas: ~$140–$160 (unframed)
  • 12×16×7/8 Gessobord: ~ $335 (unframed), depending on complexity
  • Custom sizes & framing quoted on request

Care

Acrylic works are sealed for surface protection. Avoid prolonged direct sunlight and dust with a soft, dry cloth. Cradled panels hang flush; canvas ships ready to hang unless noted.
