About the artist

Robert Grantham

Austin‑based calligrapher and painter blending Copperplate & Blackletter with contemporary abstraction. Work centers on hope, resilience, and memory— handwritten lines woven into layered paint.

View Portfolio Contact / Commissions

Artist Statement

I work where disciplined letterforms meet gestural paint. My practice grew out of years of studying traditional scripts—especially Copperplate and Blackletter— and pushing them into contemporary contexts: layered grounds, veils of glaze, scumbled textures, and palimpsests of text. The pieces often surface words like misericordia, virtus, and resilientia—not as slogans, but as anchors for grief, courage, and care. I call the studio ethos “hope, written by hand.”

Materials & Process

  • Surfaces: Ampersand Gessobord (7/8" cradle), canvas, and reclaimed cardboard.
  • Media: Acrylics (with glazes & scumbles), occasional tempera and mixed media markers.
  • Lettering: Copperplate, Blackletter, and calligraffiti hybrids built on sacred‑geometry grids.
  • Palette: Deep blues, bone whites, metallic accents, and quiet neutrals that read like aged plaster.

Themes

Much of my work wrestles with resilience—personal and communal—while tracking the marks of time. Magnolia motifs, Latin mottos, and German phrases recur as talismans. I’m interested in how handwriting—our most human mark—carries memory across surfaces and years.

Commissions

I take a limited number of commissions each month—ranging from compact calligraphy studies to larger statement works on Gessobord. If you have a word, text, or concept that matters to you, I’ll develop a layered design with sketches, script studies, and a materials plan. Student and community discounts are available when possible.

Pricing (guidance)

Pricing reflects square‑inch value, materials, and complexity. Typical ranges:

  • 8×10×5/8 canvas: ~$140–$160 (unframed).
  • 12×16×7/8 Gessobord: ~$335 (unframed), depending on complexity.
  • Custom sizes and framing quoted on request.

Note: Sliding scale or student pricing may be available on select works.

Care

Acrylic works are sealed for surface protection. Avoid prolonged direct sunlight and wipe gently with a soft dry cloth. Cradled panels hang flush; canvas pieces ship ready to hang unless otherwise noted.
